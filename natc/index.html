New Approaches toward Computing

Symposium, 9 November 2001

Academy House, Brussels

This one-day symposium has as goal to expose Belgian computer scientists (and anyone else interested) to two major new approaches to computing. Four eminent scholars will give expository presentations on Quantum Computing and DNA Computing.

Morning session: Quantum Computing

10-11: Marshall Stoneham (University College London)

11.30-12.30: Harry Buhrman (University of Amsterdam, CWI)

Afternoon session: DNA Computing

14-15: John Reif (Duke University)

15.30-16.30: Grzegorz Rozenberg (Leiden University)

How to participate

Be there! Attendance is free to everyone interested. Drinks will be provided during the breaks.

Lunch

It is possible to register for a lunch on site. This requires a fee of 500BEF to be paid on site. Registration is by email to jan.vandenbussche@luc.ac.be with mention of name and address.

How to get there

The address of the Academy House is Hertogsstraat 1, Brussels. A brochure on how to get to the Academy House is available in PDF format.
